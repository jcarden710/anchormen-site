Week 2 2025
LIVE

League members, gather round — the pigskin has officially taken flight! Week 1 of both the NFL and our humble little fantasy circus is in the books, and what a start it was. The defending champion Eagles opened the season with lightning, thunder, and just enough grit to edge out Dallas, while Josh Allen decided to audition for Hercules in Buffalo’s 41-40 comeback over Baltimore. Even rookies got in on the action — J.J. McCarthy led the Vikings to a late rally that had Minnesota fans thinking, “Wait…is this what competent quarterbacking feels like?” Out in Indy, Daniel Jones marched the Colts up and down the field like a man who forgot he was supposed to be Daniel Jones, putting Miami in a blender. In short: chaos, drama, glory, and yes — delicious television ratings that prove America is once again head over heels for football.

And in our league? The fireworks were no less dazzling. James Westfall came out guns blazing with Josh Allen at the helm, Totally Unprepared proved shockingly well-prepared by dropping the week’s high score, and Poop Mouth squeaked out a win uglier than a Champ Kind necktie. Even Brick would’ve noticed the air felt different — crisp, tense, full of the kind of possibility you can only get in Week 1. One win doesn’t make a season, but it sure makes a statement. Consider the gauntlet thrown, gentlemen and gentlewomen: the race for fantasy immortality has begun. Stay classy… or at least stay afloat.

Ladies and gentlemen of the league, it’s time for the true north star of our little fantasy universe to make its 2025 debut: the ratings. Forget your draft day hype, your lucky matchups, even that one miracle catch that bailed you out — the ratings are gospel. They're the cold, unflinching data that tells us who’s winning the hearts, minds, and eyeballs of this league. They do not care about your excuses, your “bad beat” stories, or how many bench points you left stranded. They simply are. 

And what a debut for the 2025 season. Totally Unprepared, slapped with a D+ in the draft grades and practically laughed out of the newsroom, exploded onto the scene with the week’s top score and now sits alone at the mountaintop with a flawless 1.000 AGS%. That’s a ratings darling if I’ve ever seen one. Meanwhile, preseason darlings James Westfall and An Old, Old Wooden Ship backed up their respectable draft marks with strong Week 1 performances, translating hype into prime-time placement. At the other end of the spectrum, Blackbeard’s Delight and Ubulus Muscle are gasping for air, looking more like late-night reruns than headline acts. The biggest takeaway? Week 1 reminded us that draft grades are just the critics’ preview — but once the lights go on, only the ratings tell the truth.

 

AGAINST ALL ODDS
with Brian Fantana

Folks, sometimes winning isn’t about being the biggest, the fastest, or even the smartest. Sometimes it’s about stumbling into the bar at 2 a.m., smelling like Sex Panther, and somehow convincing the bartender you belong in VIP. That was Poop Mouth in Week 1: ugly, inefficient, but victorious nonetheless. With a modest 103.17 points — barely edging out Blackbeard’s Delight — they scraped together a win that will not be remembered for style, but for sheer survival. Honestly, if victories came with a smell, this one would’ve cleared out the newsroom. Their draft was forgettable, their performance was pedestrian, and yet by some cosmic accident they walked out with a W. But you know what? Survival still gets you in the standings. So while the critics scoff, the ratings groan, and most of us wonder how long this charade can last, Poop Mouth walks away 1-0. And in this league, that’s the only fragrance that matters — even if it stinks.
