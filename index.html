Week 4 2025
LIVE

Week 3 of the NFL was wild, weird, and wonderful. The Detroit Lions punched the Ravens in the mouth on Monday night, storming out with a 38–30 upset that has Motor City puffing its chest. The Eagles staged a comeback worthy of prime-time television, erasing a 19-point hole with A.J. Brown shredding corners and a big man touchdown off a blocked field goal. In Minnesota, the Vikings humiliated the Bengals 48–10, thanks to Isaiah Rodgers turning defense into offense — twice.

 And just like that, the shake-ups echoed in our fantasy world. Metal & Brawn blasted their way to the top spot with a 163-point eruption, flexing as another  of the league’s early juggernauts. James Westfall kept the undefeated record intact at 3–0, but the air of invincibility is thinning as the score wasn't quite as sky-high. It seems Panda Watch has finally arrived, using their remodeled roster to claw past Katow-Jo and take a ratings leap, proving trades aren’t just for show. Poop Mouth suddenly found its bite, Totally Unprepared rebounded in style, and Blackbeard’s Delight kept taking on water at the bottom. The season’s picture is coming into focus: contenders on top, stragglers scrambling, and a whole lot of chaos still brewing. As always, it's anyone's year, and with no undefeated teams it feels like any could make a run.

The ratings board has turned into a roller coaster, and Week 3 did not spare anyone from the ride. Metal & Brawn roared into the #1 spot after a 163-point showcase that had the rest of the league staring up in awe — a four-place climb that stamped them as the early juggernaut. James Westfall stayed steady at #2 and remains the league’s only unbeaten team, but the glow of perfection is dimming as his margins shrink week by week.

Behind them, Panda Watch stole the spotlight, leaping six full spots after their trades finally bore fruit in an upset over Katow-Jo. That catapult came at Katow-Jo’s expense, who slid four places after a deflating loss, showing just how volatile the middle tier can be. Ubulus Muscle flexed just enough to hold position, while Poop Mouth finally found its voice, cracking 133 points and climbing back into the conversation after two weeks in the shadows.

On the downside, Totally Unprepared managed to stop the free fall with a much-needed win, but the five-spot drop from last week still looms large. Tits McGee lurks quietly in the middle, dangerous if his spending spree yields results, and Blackbeard’s Delight remains chained to the bottom — more shipwreck than ship at this point. Three weeks in, the top dogs are separating, the stragglers are gasping, and the middle looks like a mosh pit.

 

Power Plays
with Champ Kind

Week 3 had more fireworks than a Fourth of July telecast, and three teams lit up the sky in very different ways. Let’s hit the tape.

Metal & Brawn

At 163.69 points, they didn’t just win, they steamrolled. Jalen Hurts and Tyreek Hill looked like a highlight package come to life, and the depth behind them was enough to make every other roster in the league sweat bullets. That’s not just a win, folks — that’s a statement.

Panda Watch

You thought two straight losses and a roster shake-up spelled doom? Wrong! They came out swinging in Week 3, knocking off Katow-Jo and vaulting six places in the standings. That’s like going from the kids’ table to carving the turkey. Trades paid off, and suddenly Panda Watch isn’t just doom and gloom — they’re contenders.

Poop Mouth

The record says 1–2, but don’t be fooled. Dropping 133.31 points in a loss makes them the most dangerous underdog in town. Mahomes and Kamara finally looked like stars, and if the supporting cast keeps showing up, this is a powder keg waiting to go off. Ignore them now, regret it later.
