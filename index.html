The Corningstone Report: Anchormen Superlatives

As we head into draft weekend, it’s important to take stock of where each franchise truly stands. History, consistency, and yes, a little bit of hardware, all play a role in separating the contenders from the pretenders. These tiers aren’t about what your roster might look like on Sunday night — they’re about what your résumé says heading into Season 14.

Tier 1: The Usual Headlines
In a league built on chaos, some names keep showing up on the front page. These are the franchises you expect to see when the stakes are highest.
Panda Watch — Consistent winners who treat playoffs like routine.
Blackbeard's Delight — Regular-season juggernaut, still allergic to trophies.

Tier 2 — In the News, Not the Lead
This group knows how to make noise, but hasn’t quite delivered the Pulitzer-worthy ending. Strong résumés, yes, but there’s always a “but” waiting in the copy.
Metal and Brawn — Once a dynasty, now proving they’re still relevant.
James Westfall — Always close, but four straight Burg losses sting.
Poop Mouth — A Burg to their name, but playoff nightmares persist.
Ubulus Muscle — Strong debut, but one year doesn’t make history.

Tier 3: Below the Fold
Stories worth reading, but rarely above the crease. These teams either can’t find consistency, are still building credibility, or, frankly, just don’t know how to stick the landing.
An Old, Old Wooden Ship — Reliable middle of the pack, rarely more.
Katow Jo — A Burg in the archives, but little else.
Totally Unprepared — Last year’s miracle, still short on credibility.
Tits McGee — Known more for headlines than results.

This has been Veronica Corningstone, reminding you: anyone can dream on draft day, but only a few will make the front page when it’s all over.
