The pendulum swings, the ticker spins, and Burgundy’s mustache twitches in anticipation — welcome to Week 3.

League Superlatives
with Veronica Corningstone
Most Improved: Katow-Jo

Katow-Jo looked flat in Week 1, with Amon-Ra St. Brown and Nico Collins both failing to crack double digits and the backfield sputtering to barely 20 points. One week later, the switch flipped. St. Brown exploded for 39.2, Tucker Kraft turned into Travis Kelce-lite with 24.4, and even the supporting cast of Jaxon Smith-Njigba and Tetairoa McMillan showed up with big games. The 113.22 opener suddenly looks like a bad dress rehearsal; the 173.3 in Week 2 was the real premiere. If this is the version of Katow-Jo we’re going to see more often, the rest of the league should start circling their matchups in pencil — and praying.

Biggest Letdown: Totally Unprepared

Meanwhile, Totally Unprepared played the role of Icarus — soaring high in Week 1 before crashing spectacularly back to earth. The trio of Derrick Henry, Christian McCaffrey, and Baker Mayfield carried them to glory with over 75 combined points to start the season. One week later? That same trio managed less than 45, highlighted by Henry’s miserable 2.3-point dud. The supporting cast offered no bailout, with Sutton and Etienne combining for just 3.2 points. The 56-point collapse from 159.38 to 102.75 was more than a stumble — it was a faceplant, one that dropped them five spots in the rankings. Week 1 hinted at dominance; Week 2 made them look, well, exactly as their team name suggests.
